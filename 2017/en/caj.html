Abril Training Program

Introduction

Abril Training Program is an initiative from Editora Abril, a major Brazilian publisher company and one of the biggest in Latin America, that is highly selective and has an acceptance rate of 2%.

We were divided into groups of text, photography, video, digital media, illustration and design.

During the program I was a part of group responsible for making an article about Usain Bolt for Veja magazine, the leading weekly publication in the country. I also spent one week working inside its head quarters and learned about the daily routine of one of the most influential outlets of the Brazilian printed media.

Details

Video application

We were required to make a video application for the admission process answering the question: “Who am I and why do I want to work with editorial design?”. After failing at speaking in front of a camera, I had to try something else, so I made a video explaining what I believe is the designer role when it comes to journalism: transform a plain text into something more attractive and easier to understand.

VEJA project

Among great athletes such as Katie Ledecky and Dennis Kimetto, we chose Usain Bolt as the subject of our article about athletes with outstanding performances.

I worked with Iara Adeodato as the design team responsible for coming up with a mobile-focused online article respecting the VEJA magazine identity.
